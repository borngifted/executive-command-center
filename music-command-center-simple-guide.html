The big idea

A smart helper for the boss

  • It keeps all the important music-business information together.
  • It checks songs, contracts, ownership, payments, and reports.
  • It shows a warning before a small mistake becomes a big problem.
  • You can click the warning and go straight to what needs fixing.
Why it matters

Small mistakes can quietly eat the money.

Nothing may look broken today. Months later, the company may discover a song had no contract, the owners claimed too much or too little, or a report used pretend numbers.

📄

Missing paper

A song leaves the house before everyone signs the permission slips.

🧩

Pieces do not fit

The ownership pieces should make one whole pie: exactly 100%.

🧮

Bad math

A report says more items were sold than the company ever had.

The five screens

Five simple rooms in one control center

Choose a button to see what each screen does.

1

Overview — the car dashboard

Like looking at the speed, gas, and warning lights in a car.

  • See artists, songs, releases, and money in one place.
  • See the most important problems first.
  • Click a warning to open the exact problem.
Warning: “Midnight” is missing its ISRC.
Child-sized meaning: the song has no serial number, so the music store cannot identify it.
2

Approvals — permission before launch

Like a teacher checking your field-trip form before you get on the bus.

  • Green check = ready.
  • Red X = something is missing.
  • The boss can still say “go,” but must confirm the risk.
  • Every yes or no gets a dated receipt.
Receipt: A. Reeves approved “First Light” with one open risk. No one has to guess later.
3

Artists & Rights — who owns each piece?

A song is a pizza. Every owner's slice must add up to the whole pizza.

  • The total must be exactly 100%.
  • The system checks whether signed proof is on file.
  • An A–F grade shows how protected the artist is.

4

Numbers Check — does the math tell the truth?

You cannot sell 12 cookies if you only baked 10.

  • Test 1: Is the math even possible?
  • Test 2: Did listeners become real paying customers?
  • Big numbers are not automatically good numbers.
Demo example: “Fast Money” says 61,000 sales, but only 50,000 units shipped. That is impossible. It also has 5.1 million streams but only 360 purchases.
5

Trends & Radio — are we growing?

Like marking your height on a wall to see if you got taller.

  • A line shows whether streams rise or fall each month.
  • A funnel shows listeners → savers → buyers.
  • A radio board shows where a song was played.
  • Every report says where it came from and how it was checked.

ISRC

UPC

The barcode for the full package: a single, EP, or album.

Splits

How the song money is divided. All pieces must total 100%.

Master

The actual recorded performance of the song.

Publishing

The ownership of the song's words and melody.

✓

Split sheet

The signed paper proving everyone agreed to their percentage.

Spin

One time a radio station or show plays the song.

A never-forgetting receipt book for important decisions.
